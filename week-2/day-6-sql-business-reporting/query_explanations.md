# Query Explanations - Day 6 SQL Business Reporting Sprint

This document explains important SQL queries created during the Day 6 practice and the business purpose behind each report.

---

## Query title: Count all orders

**File:** basic_aggregations.sql

**Business question:** How many total orders exist in the system?

**Tables used:** orders

**Why JOIN is needed:** A JOIN is not needed because all required information exists inside the orders table.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE is not needed because we want to count every order regardless of status.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY is not needed because the result should return one total number.

**What I understood:** This query measures the total activity level of the business by showing how many orders exist.

---

## Query title: Completed revenue

**File:** basic_aggregations.sql

**Business question:** How much confirmed revenue was generated from completed orders?

**Tables used:** orders and products

**Why JOIN is needed:** The orders table contains product_id and quantity, while the products table contains the price. JOIN connects these tables using product_id.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE filters only completed orders because pending and cancelled orders are not confirmed revenue.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY is not needed because we only need one total revenue value.

**What I understood:** Revenue calculations must exclude unfinished orders to avoid showing incorrect business performance.

## Query title: Count orders by status

**File:** group_by_reports.sql

**Business question:** How many orders exist for each status?

**Tables used:** orders

**Why JOIN is needed:** A JOIN is not needed because status information is already stored in the orders table.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE is not needed because all statuses should be included in the report.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY creates separate results for each status value.

**What I understood:** This report helps managers understand how many orders are completed, pending, or cancelled.

## Query title: Completed revenue by product_id

**File:** group_by_reports.sql

**Business question:** How much completed revenue was generated by each product ID?

**Tables used:** orders and products

**Why JOIN is needed:** The orders table stores product_id and quantity, while products stores the price required for revenue calculation.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE keeps only completed orders because only completed transactions count as revenue.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY creates one revenue result for each product_id.

**What I understood:** This report shows which products generate the highest revenue and helps with product performance analysis.

## Query title: Completed revenue by category

**File:** join_reports.sql

**Business question:** Which product category generated the most completed revenue?

**Tables used:** orders and products

**Why JOIN is needed:** Orders contains product_id, while products contains category and price information.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE filters only completed orders because pending and cancelled orders are not real revenue.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY creates one result row for each category.

**What I understood:** Electronics generated the highest completed revenue because expensive products such as laptops and monitors contribute more value.

## Query title: Order count by city

**File:** join_reports.sql

**Business question:** Which cities have the highest number of customer orders?

**Tables used:** orders and customers

**Why JOIN is needed:** Orders contains customer_id, while customers contains city information. JOIN connects these tables using customer_id.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE is not needed because all orders should be included.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY combines orders by city and calculates the number of orders for each location.

**What I understood:** This report helps compare customer activity between different cities.

## Query title: Top 3 customers by completed revenue

**File:** join_reports.sql

**Business question:** Which customers generated the highest completed revenue?

**Tables used:** orders, customers, and products

**Why JOIN is needed:** Customer names come from customers, while quantity and price needed for revenue calculations come from orders and products.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE filters only completed orders because only successful sales count as revenue.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY calculates revenue separately for every customer.

**What I understood:** This query identifies the most valuable customers and can support customer retention decisions.

---

## Query title: Pending and cancelled orders potential value

**File:** join_reports.sql

**Business question:** What is the potential value of orders that are not completed?

**Tables used:** orders, customers, and products

**Why JOIN is needed:** JOIN combines customer information, product information, and prices needed to calculate potential amount.

**Why WHERE is needed:** WHERE filters only pending and cancelled orders.

**Why GROUP BY is needed:** GROUP BY is not needed because this report displays individual orders instead of summaries.

**What I understood:** Pending and cancelled orders represent possible future value, but they should not be included in confirmed revenue reports.
